Modern Manners & Mental Fortitude

The life-skills course your students actually need. This mobile version turns the one-pager into readable sections instead of a tiny poster image.

Grades7-12
Duration16 Weeks
Format2x / Week
Class Size20-25
Overview

What this course is for

Academic achievement dominates most curricula while students navigate complex social, digital, and emotional landscapes with too few real tools. This course makes those tools explicit, measurable, and transferable for school, family, workplace, and community life.

Four Core Pillars

01 Respectful Communication

Build steady, clear, respectful communication across real pressure moments.

02 Emotional Regulation

Practice calming, labeling, reframing, and responding on purpose.

03 Decision-Making

Make stronger choices under pressure by thinking through options and consequences.

04 Accountability & Repair

Learn how to own mistakes, restore trust, and repair damage well.

Five Course Modules

Module 01 Modern Manners

Daily respect, presence, and social awareness in real settings.

Module 02 Emotional Intelligence

Recognize emotion, regulate it, and keep judgment intact.

Module 03 Conflict Navigation

Handle tension, peer pressure, and misunderstandings with control.

Module 04 Digital Citizenship

Protect reputation, privacy, and long-term opportunity online.

Module 05 Personal Growth

Build responsibility, maturity, and identity that transfers beyond class.

Two Student Frameworks

Regulation Routine

Pause • Label • Reframe • Respond

Introduced in Module 02 and reinforced across all modules. It teaches self-regulation as a performance skill, not a therapy tool.

Decision Framework

Pause • Options • Consequences • Choose

Introduced in Module 03 and reinforced across all modules. Students apply it to real and simulated high-stakes scenarios.

Who This Course Serves

Grades 7-8

Foundational, one-term introduction to all five modules and both frameworks.

Grades 9-12

Depth increases each year. Students build on prior knowledge through applied scenarios.

Community Youth

After-school programs and faith organizations. Adaptable for non-school settings.

Community Adults

Workplace teams and family development contexts. Skills transfer directly to adult life.

How Learning Is Measured

Weekly Journals

Capture reflection, application, and personal growth over time.

Role-Play Rubrics

Assess live use of communication, regulation, and accountability skills.

Pre & Post Surveys

Show growth in self-awareness, confidence, and real-life transfer.

Capstone & Novel Scenario Task

Demonstrate skills in new situations where there is no rehearsed script.

What Students Will Be Able To Do
  • Apply respectful communication across school, workplace, and digital contexts.
  • Make structured decisions under pressure using the Decision Framework.
  • Own mistakes, repair relationships, and follow through on commitments.
  • Self-regulate using the Regulation Routine as a performance skill.
  • Navigate digital life with permanence, privacy, and reputation in mind.
  • Transfer skills independently to unfamiliar, high-stakes real-world situations.
